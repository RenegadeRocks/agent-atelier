## How it started

Two siblings — Meethi (26, quit a Gurgaon ad job) and Aarav (23, dropped out of a CA
course with zero regrets) — spent the 2024 heatwave on their Jaipur terrace with a
₹4,000 popsicle mould set and a crate of Safeda mangoes their uncle couldn't sell
before they turned. The first batch was mango, lime, and a pinch of salt. The
neighbours' kids climbed the wall for seconds. The building WhatsApp group placed
orders for Friday. That was the whole business plan.

Chuski Club makes small-batch fruit ice pops — chuskis, grown up but never boring.
Real fruit bought ripe at dawn from the mandi, pressed and frozen the same day, in
flavours that follow the Rajasthan seasons: Safeda mango, phalsa (two glorious weeks
a year), kala khatta, rose-falooda, guava-chilli, imli-banta. No syrupy neon, no
year-old freezer stock. When a fruit's season ends, the pop retires until next year —
and the retirement post is a running joke followers genuinely mourn over.

## What we actually sell

1. **The Summer Dozen** — a mixed box of twelve pops, delivered frozen inside Jaipur
   the same evening you order. The box art changes monthly; people collect them.
2. **The Pop Cart** — our pink-and-mango cart, booked for birthdays, school fetes,
   haldis and mehendis. Two hours, unlimited pops, one very tired scooter.

## Who it's for

The 23-year-old who grew up on ₹2 chuskis outside the school gate and now has a
salary, a terrace, and friends coming over on Saturday. Office teams melting through
a Jaipur June. Mothers who want the birthday party to look incredible on camera
without ordering a fondant cake shaped like a phone. People who send the retirement
post to a friend with "NOOO phalsa season is over".

## How we talk

Sunny, quick, a little cheeky. We say "it's hot, come eat ice" instead of "beat the
heat with artisanal frozen refreshment". We name the fruit, the mandi morning, the
exact colour your tongue turns. We never guilt anyone about sugar, never pretend a
popsicle is a health food, never punch at other brands. If a line wouldn't make your
younger cousin grin, cut it.

Lines we love: "Phalsa season lasts fourteen days. Cancel your plans." · "Your tongue
is going to be purple in four minutes." · "Mango o'clock is 4pm sharp."

Lines we'd never post: "Indulge guilt-free in our sugar-conscious creations." ·
"❄️🔥 BEAT THE HEAT NOW — LIMITED STOCK!!"

## What it looks like

Hard Jaipur sunshine, saturated candy colour, pink city walls, a drip racing down
someone's wrist, tongues mid-laugh, the cart's brass bell, crushed ice flying. Bright,
sun-drenched, joyful, a little chaotic — never moody, never dim, never "premium dark".
If a photo looks like a whisky ad, it's not ours. Our colours: hot pink, Safeda
yellow, mint, and white like a fresh kulfi stick.

## Texture we keep coming back to

The 4pm loo wind rattling the terrace door. Bapu Bazaar's pink walls at golden hour.
The school van horn at 1:40pm. Safeda crates stacked at Muhana Mandi before sunrise.
The old kulfi cart bell at Chaupar. Haldi-yellow wedding tents in November. Power-cut
evenings solved on the terrace with a box of pops. The phalsa lady who saves us the
first crate. Cane-juice crushers on Tonk Road. The freezer's frost bloom when you
open it at noon. A purple kala-khatta tongue in a selfie. The rooftop water tank
that's bath-warm by ten.

## Where we are

Jaipur only, for now — delivery inside the city, the cart within 25km. Instagram is
the shop window: @chuskiclub. Orders by DM ("MELT") or WhatsApp. Hindi and English,
mixed the way Jaipur actually speaks.
