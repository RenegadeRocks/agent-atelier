## About us

Kanva Coffee Works is a small-batch roastery in Bengaluru. We started in 2021 in a garage
off Old Airport Road with a secondhand 5-kilo roaster and one conviction: the coffee grown
three hours from this city is world-class, and almost nobody in the city has tasted it the
way it can taste.

We buy from four family estates around Chikkamagaluru and Sakleshpur — the same families
every season, at rates we're proud of — and we roast twice a week, in batches small enough
that the person who roasted your beans wrote the date on your bag by hand. Founder Meera
Kanvar spent nine years as a food scientist before trading a lab coat for a roastery
apron; her co-founder (and uncle) Ravi grew up on one of the estates we now buy from.

We are not a "third-wave concept space." We are a working roastery that believes filter
kaapi culture and specialty coffee are cousins, not rivals — the steel tumbler and the
V60 sit on the same shelf here.

## What we sell

**The Roastery Subscription** *(our flagship)* — 250g or 500g of the current small-batch
roast, delivered monthly anywhere in India, roasted within 72 hours of dispatch. Every bag
names its estate, elevation, varietal, process, and roast date. Subscribers get the
roaster's notes: what we tasted, what we changed, what to try brewing.

**First Crack Sundays** — a 3-hour hands-on brewing workshop at the roastery, last Sunday
of every month, twelve seats. Cupping, grind-size play, a filter-kaapi-vs-pour-over
side-by-side, and everyone roasts a small tray to take home. Most of our long-term
subscribers started as workshop walk-ins.

## What we believe

- **The grower's name belongs on the bag.** Every bag names the estate and the family.
- **Fresh beats fancy.** A ₹450 bag roasted last Tuesday beats a ₹2,000 imported bag
  roasted four months ago. Roast date over everything.
- **Coffee is a craft, not a gate.** If you brew in a steel dabarah with chicory, you are
  our people. If you own a scale and a gooseneck, you are our people.
- **Say it plainly.** We will never tell you a coffee has "notes of Ecuadorian moonlight."
  We'll tell you it tastes like sweet lime, jaggery, and a good morning.
