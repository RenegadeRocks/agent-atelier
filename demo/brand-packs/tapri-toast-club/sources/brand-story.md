# Tapri Toast Club — brand story

*(Fictional demo brand. Any resemblance to a real business is coincidental.)*

Tapri Toast Club is a chai-and-toast counter on a corner near Sarafa, Indore.
It started in 2023 when two flatmates — Ishaan (an ex-line-cook) and Mitali (a
lapsed graphic designer) — got tired of cafés charging ₹340 for sourdough that
tasted like regret, and decided the greatest food pairing in India was already
perfect: cutting chai and butter toast off a tawa. They just wanted to do it
*properly*.

Properly means: bread from a slow overnight dough baked in-house every morning,
butter that is actually butter, a masala-chai brew timed with a sand timer
(flip it twice, never three times), and a toast menu that changes with what's
good at the mandi — corn-cheese in monsoon, matar-masala in winter, aam-shrikhand
toast for exactly three weeks in June.

The counter has six stools, a butter-yellow wall Mitali painted herself, and a
rule written on a chalkboard: **"Phones down while the toast is hot."** Nobody
follows it. The regulars are students from Holkar College, night-shift coders,
and one retired judge who has occupied stool #4 at 7:40 AM since opening day.

The name is a joke that stuck: a "tapri" is the humblest possible tea stall,
and "club" is the least humble word in English. That collision — street-corner
food with unreasonable standards — is the whole brand.

What Tapri Toast Club believes:

- Chai is a schedule, not a beverage. The 8 AM cup and the 5 PM cup are
  different religions.
- Butter is a food group and we will not be taking questions.
- A toast should crunch loud enough that the person on the next stool turns
  around.
- Fancy is not the same as good. Good is good.

The vibe is young, loud, slightly unhinged about bread, and fiercely local to
Indore — a city that takes eating more seriously than any city in India and
has the 2 AM Sarafa food street to prove it.

They sell: the daily toast menu at the counter, a "Toast Subscription" (a
punch card — nine toasts, tenth free), and a weekend "Tawa Box" of assembly-
at-home toast kits for house parties.
