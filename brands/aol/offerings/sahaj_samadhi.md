## 1. What it is

A truthful, concrete description of Sahaj Samadhi Meditation in Art of Living Ludhiana's own terms.

- **Plain definition:** Sahaj Samadhi Meditation is a mantra-based meditation technique taught by Art of Living. "Sahaj" means effortless in Sanskrit; "Samadhi" refers to a natural state of awareness beyond waking, sleeping, and dreaming — described as "an unlimited reservoir of energy, intelligence and creative power." Practitioners use a personal, charged subtle sound (Beeja mantra) as a vehicle to settle the mind into stillness without force or concentration.
- **Category / form:** multi-session course (3 consecutive days), then a self-sustaining daily home practice. This brand is `educational`; the hero is a concept/feeling image, not a product.
- **The heart of it:** the personal **Beeja (seed) mantra** — a charged subtle sound from the Vedic tradition, received from a certified teacher and used silently and effortlessly. Remove it and this becomes a different practice. Once learned, Sahaj Samadhi is an independent, portable skill: the meditator needs only a quiet space and 20 minutes — no ongoing instruction, app, or audio.
- **What it is NOT:** not Sudarshan Kriya (that is rhythmic breathwork); not guided meditation (no ongoing spoken instruction); not concentration or mindfulness meditation (no sustained mental effort, thought-watching, or focus). Do not conflate it with Sudarshan Kriya in copy.

> **Accuracy contract.** Every line here must survive the owner reading it aloud. The practice is rooted in the ancient Vedic mantra tradition and taught exclusively by teachers personally certified by Gurudev Sri Sri Ravi Shankar. Let the §7 angles make it compelling — never a softened or exaggerated definition.

## 3. Accurate description (the deeper knowledge the agent reasons from)

### 3.1 How it actually works / what happens

Mantra-based (effortless) meditation. Each participant receives a **personal Beeja (seed) mantra** from a certified teacher during a traditional initiation ceremony. "Beeja" means seed; these charged subtle sounds may carry no literal meaning. The meditator gently introduces the mantra and allows the mind to settle naturally — the mantra functions as a vehicle, not an object of concentration. It requires no effort, no concentration, and no guidance once learned.

**Non-disclosure rule (binds words AND image):** the Sahaj Samadhi mantra — and how it is received or repeated — is **never** revealed, in words or image. The mantra is held privately by tradition: not shared with others, not discussed, never depicted. Describe the *experience* of settling into stillness, never the mechanics.

**Daily practice:** twice daily (morning and evening recommended), 20 minutes per session, quiet comfortable seated position, preferably before meals or 90+ minutes after eating. Once learned it requires no instructor, app, or audio guidance.

**Key distinction from Sudarshan Kriya:** Sudarshan Kriya uses specific rhythmic breathing patterns tied to natural biological rhythms; Sahaj Samadhi uses silent mantra repetition in seated stillness. The two are complementary but structurally different. Do not conflate them.

### 3.2 What makes it distinct

Effortless and self-sustaining: unlike guided meditations that require ongoing instruction, Sahaj Samadhi becomes an independent, portable skill once learned. Taught exclusively by teachers personally certified by Gurudev Sri Sri Ravi Shankar, preserving the authenticity of the transmission. (No comparative framing — `comparative_claims_allowed` is false; never "better than other meditations.")

### 3.3 Where it sits in the brand's ladder

It deepens and follows The Happiness Program. Sudarshan Kriya (breath-led) and Sahaj Samadhi (mantra-led, seated) are complementary; many practitioners hold both. This offering is introduced as the natural next step for a matured graduate, not as a louder or "advanced" pitch.

> **Never trivialized.** Do not make it sound easy/quick/magic. Describe the real thing; let the §7 angles make it compelling.
