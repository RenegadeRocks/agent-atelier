## 1. What Instagram is for, for us

Instagram is our **public doorway**. People who don't yet know [[BRAND_NAME]] ([[BRAND_SHORT_NAME]]) scroll past. We are competing with reels, not with longform readers. The job is to **stop the scroll, land one idea, and leave a door open** — not to teach the whole of [[MISSION]] in one post.

A good IG piece does *less* than a good longform piece. That is the discipline. Hold the [[SCROLL_TEST_PERSONA]] in mind: if it wouldn't stop their thumb, it isn't an IG piece yet.

## 2. Caption length

**Target: 80–120 words. Hard cap: 150 words.**

Anything over 150 words is **reject on sight** — send it back for compression, not edits. The caption is failing the channel, not the writing.

- 80–120 is the rhythm that reads on a phone, above and just-below the fold.
- 150 is the absolute ceiling. If a piece needs more, it's a longform piece or a carousel — not a single caption.
- Word count includes hashtags only if they sit inside the caption body. Hashtags placed in the first comment or in a trailing block don't count (and per §5 they should never be in the body).

### How to measure

Whitespace-split words. Hyphenated compounds count as one. Terms in any of [[LANGUAGES]] count as one word each. Em-dash chunks count separately. When in doubt, paste into a word counter — don't eyeball.

## 3. Job count — pick 2 to 3, never all five

Every caption is built from some subset of these five jobs:

1. **Hook** — the reframe in the first one or two lines.
2. **Quote / source citation** — a founder, lineage, or canonical-source line, *with its source*, per [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]] and [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]].
3. **Evidence citation** — a hedged, named external reference, drawn only from [[SOURCE_ALLOWLIST]] (never [[SOURCE_DENYLIST]]) and limited to [[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]].
4. **Practical instruction** — *do this today*; one small, concrete action.
5. **CTA** — an invitation tied to an offering from [[OFFERINGS]] or to [[CONTACT_WHATSAPP]] / [[CONTACT_INSTAGRAM]], in the [[CTA_STYLE]] register.

**Pick 2–3. Never all five.**

Doing all five at full length is the classic failure mode — the caption becomes a compressed essay instead of a scroll-stopper. If you find yourself writing all five, the right move is not to shorten each — it is to **drop two**.

Suggested pairings that work on IG:

- **Hook + practical instruction** (highest performer for evergreen).
- **Hook + quote** (good for founder / canonical-source days).
- **Hook + evidence citation + soft CTA** (good for the evidence-curious; CTA must be soft, claims within [[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]]).
- **Hook + practical instruction + CTA** (offering-driven posts).

If a piece truly needs all five jobs, **make it a carousel** — see §6.

## 4. Caption rhythm

- **First 1–2 lines must do the hook.** Above the fold ≈ the first ~125 characters before *…more*. If the reframe lives in paragraph two, you've buried it.
- **Line breaks, not essay paragraphs.** Single-line beats. Blank line between beats. We are writing for thumbs, not for a magazine column.
- **No question stacking.** One opening question is fine. Three questions in a row reads as anxious copywriting.
- **No "wait for it" / clickbait hooks.** The voice is [[VOICE_DESCRIPTORS]] — invitational, never bait. Keep to [[VOICE_DO]]; avoid [[VOICE_DONT]].
- **Reading level** stays at [[READING_LEVEL]].
- **Code-switching** across [[LANGUAGES]] is welcome where it sounds like [[LOCALE]]. Don't force it; don't avoid it.

### Reject on sight

- Hook hidden after the quote or the evidence.
- Essay paragraphs with no line breaks.
- Em-dash overload as a stylistic crutch (AI-slop tell — see `../brand_voice.md`, AI-slop section).
- Generic filler openers/closers ("In today's fast-paced world…", "Let's dive in…", "In conclusion…") and any phrasing in [[VOICE_DONT]] or [[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]].

## 5. Hashtag policy

**[[CHANNELS]]-appropriate count: 8–12 hashtags. Always in the first comment, not in the caption body.**

- Mix of three tiers:
  - **2–3 broad** — wide topic tags for the [[BRAND_TYPE]] space.
  - **3–5 brand / offering** — tags derived from [[BRAND_NAME]] / [[BRAND_SHORT_NAME]] and the relevant [[OFFERING_NAME]] from [[OFFERINGS]].
  - **2–4 local** — tags tied to [[LOCALE]].
- **No hashtag stuffing inside sentences** ("learn to #dothis today" — reject).
- **No more than 12.** More signals spam and dilutes reach.
- **No empty trend-chasing tags** (generic motivation / viral / explore-page tags) unless the piece is genuinely that thing.
- **Founder / figure / lineage hashtags** must obey [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]] and [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]]: never an ambiguous standalone honorific that could be confused with another entity — qualify it so attribution is unmistakable.

## 6. Format: single image vs. carousel vs. reel

The 80–120 rule is for **single-image captions and Reels captions**. Carousels relax it — but with discipline.

- **Single image** — 80–120 words, 2–3 jobs.
- **Carousel (2–10 slides)** — caption 60–100 words (slides do the heavy lifting). Slide 1 = the hook image + one line. Slide 2–N = one idea per slide. Last slide = soft CTA. If all five jobs need to land in one piece, this is the format.
- **Reel** — caption 40–80 words. The hook is in the **first ~1.5 seconds of the video**, not the caption. Caption is context + CTA. Voiceover/on-screen captions on the Reel itself follow the spoken-voice (short-video) guide.

> Channel availability for auto-publish follows the platform constraints in the PRD: feed **single-image + carousel** only at launch; Reels/Stories auto-publish is out of launch (manual handoff unaffected). Confirm the current carousel child max at build time.

## 7. Visual pairing

The **visual style guide** (`../visual_style_guide.md`) is canonical for all image decisions — load it alongside this guide for every IG piece. Load the **visual concepts** doc (`../visual_concepts.md`) as well: **concept class is chosen before subject.** Below is the IG-specific summary; the full rule set lives in those documents and in [[VISUAL_STRATEGY]] / [[VISUAL_REGISTER]] / [[VISUAL_VARIETY]].

- **Subject framing matches caption mood.** Pick the gesture/posture that embodies the caption's idea; don't decorate a caption with a generic, on-theme stock aesthetic.
- **[[LOCALE]] register.** Honor [[VISUAL_REGISTER]] and the [[LOCAL_DETAIL_BANK]] — the authentic, specific register reads better than a generic one for this audience. See the visual style guide for the full register spec and subject-diversity rules: every image must differ from the previous 2–3 on ≥2 dimensions per [[VISUAL_VARIETY]] (e.g. gender / age / clothing / posture / setting / lighting mood).
- **Anti-cues are required** in generation prompts — the explicit negatives from the visual style guide. **Rationed concept classes** are tracked: check the ledger before choosing a concept class so a rationed class isn't over-used across the feed.
- **Image generation is text-free.** Images are generated clean via [[IMAGE_PROVIDER]] (quality tier [[IMAGE_QUALITY_TIER]]) with space reserved for composited type; a **deterministic Cloud Vision OCR text-free gate** runs on the raw pre-composite image — any baked glyphs → automatic fail → regenerate. We composite all brand typography ourselves ([[HEADLINE_FONT]] / [[LABEL_FONT]], palette [[PALETTE_HEX]], [[ACCENT_DARK_BG]] / [[ACCENT_LIGHT_BG]], with [[LOGO_ASSET]] / [[WORDMARK_TEXT]] as specified).
- **Alt text is mandatory.** One sentence, descriptive, accessible — describe the actual scene (subject, dress, posture, light), not "[mood] vibes." A real example shape: "A person seated cross-legged on a low surface, hands open in their lap, soft afternoon light." First-comment hashtags and a clear first caption line carry light IG-SEO; lead with a concrete, searchable phrase, not filler.
- **No text-on-image clickbait.** All-caps "you won't believe…" style overlays → reject the whole piece.
- **Carousel slide-1 overlays:** slide 1 carries the hook image + a very short overlay (≤6 words) per the creative engine doc; the single-image overlay word-count rule does not govern carousel slides.

## 8. CTAs — what's allowed

CTAs on IG are gentle and **specific**, in the [[CTA_STYLE]] register.

- ✅ A small, concrete next action ("Try this for five minutes today.").
- ✅ A specific, real invitation tied to an [[OFFERING_NAME]] or a standing event in [[STANDING_WEEK]] at a named place in [[LOCALE]].
- ✅ A soft "DM us / [[CONTACT_WHATSAPP]] / [[CONTACT_INSTAGRAM]] if you'd like to learn more" for a relevant offering.
- ✅ "Link in bio" for a calendar or resource.

- ❌ All-caps urgency ("DM US NOW!!!").
- ❌ Fear-of-missing-out / regret framing ("Don't miss out", "you'll regret it").
- ❌ Any urgency-fabricated or coercive CTA, and anything in [[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]]. The voice is invitational, not coercive (see `../brand_voice.md`).

One CTA per caption. Two CTAs reads desperate.

> Claims inside any CTA or body copy must stay within [[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]], never touch [[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]], and honor [[COMPARATIVE_CLAIMS_ALLOWED]] and [[POLITICAL_CONTENT_ALLOWED]]. Quantitative claims follow the evidence rules in §3/§10.

## 9. Worked examples

Worked examples are **structural patterns**, not fixed copy — resolve them from [[SAMPLE_LINES_GOOD]] / [[SAMPLE_LINES_BAD]] and the active piece. The point is the *shape*, not the words.

### ✅ Passing pattern (≈110–120 words, 3 jobs: hook + quote + soft CTA)

A passing single-image caption:

- Opens with a 1–2 line **hook** that reframes a common assumption (above the fold).
- Delivers one **quote / source citation** with its source named, per [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]] and [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]].
- Closes with one **soft, specific CTA** to a real [[OFFERING_NAME]] in [[LOCALE]].
- Lands inside 80–120 words, in line breaks (not essay paragraphs), at [[READING_LEVEL]].

**Why it passes:** inside the word target; only 3 of the 5 jobs; hook above the fold; line-break rhythm; source attributed; CTA specific and invitational.

### ❌ Failing pattern (~220 words, all five jobs)

A failing caption takes the same idea but piles on **all five jobs** — hook + quote + a second quote + an evidence citation + practical instruction + CTA — run together as a wall of text with almost no line breaks.

**Why it fails:**

1. **Length.** Over the 150-word hard cap. Reject on length alone.
2. **Job overload.** Does all five jobs at once — a compressed essay, not a scroll-stopper. The fix is to *pick 2–3 and drop the rest*, not to shorten each.
3. **Rhythm.** Reads as prose, not IG beats; sentences run together.
4. **Hook recovers, then drowns.** The first two lines may land — but the citations bury them.

**Fix path.** Compress to hook + quote + soft CTA (the passing pattern above). The extra quote and the evidence citation belong on a **carousel** (one per slide) or in a **longform** piece, not in a single IG caption.

## 10. Reviewer checklist (Creative Director use)

Run every IG submission through these in order. **First fail = reject; don't keep checking after that.** This sits inside the CD's **Gate 0 (Scroll Test) → Gate 1 (compliance)** pre-render review, with the **deterministic ledger-linter** having already hard-blocked countable-rotation violations *before* this review, and the **CD post-render multimodal pass** checking the rendered artifact afterward. Safety fields ([[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]], [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]], [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]]) are **fail-closed**.

1. **Length.** 80–120 target, ≤150 hard cap. Carousel/Reel exceptions in §6.
2. **Jobs.** 2–3 of {hook, quote, evidence, instruction, CTA}. Five jobs = reject.
3. **Hook position.** First 1–2 lines, above the fold.
4. **Rhythm.** Line breaks, not essay. No question stacking. No AI-slop tells. Reading level [[READING_LEVEL]].
5. **Voice & truth.** Forbidden phrasing ([[VOICE_DONT]] / [[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]])? Source paraphrased without attribution? Claim outside [[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]] or touching [[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]]? Quantitative claim not grounded per the deterministic claim-grounding rules (allowlist [[SOURCE_ALLOWLIST]], not [[SOURCE_DENYLIST]]; second source where [[REQUIRE_SECOND_SOURCE_FOR_QUANTITATIVE]]; reverify within [[CLAIM_REVERIFY_MONTHS]] months)? → see `../brand_voice.md`.
6. **Hashtags.** 8–12, tiered, in first comment; founder/figure tags obey [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]].
7. **Visual pairing.** Subject + gesture + register match caption mood. Alt text present and concrete. Concept class named, differs from previous 2 posts per [[VISUAL_VARIETY]]; rationed-class ledger check done; text-free OCR gate passed.
8. **CTA.** ≤1, specific, invitational, in [[CTA_STYLE]]. No urgency fabrication.

Pass all eight → `approve`. Any fail → `revise` with the specific line note and the rule number. Round 3 → escalate. **Compliant-but-dead is a reject.**
