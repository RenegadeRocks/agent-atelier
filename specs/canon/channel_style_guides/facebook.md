# [[BRAND_NAME]] — Facebook Style Guide

> Channel-specific rules that sit on top of the **brand voice doc** (`../brand_voice.md`). Load both before drafting or reviewing a Facebook piece. **Hard gate for standing Facebook routines.**
>
> Anything not contradicted here defers to `../brand_voice.md`. If you can't find a rule, the rule is in the voice doc. The **brand voice is identical across channels** — Facebook changes the *mechanics* (length, links, hashtag norm, format mix), never the voice.

## 1. What Facebook is for, for us

Facebook is our **community room**, not our shop window. The Instagram doorway (`./instagram.md`) competes with Reels for a stranger's thumb; Facebook is where people who already lean toward [[BRAND_NAME]] ([[BRAND_SHORT_NAME]]) **read a little longer, share to their own circle, and start a conversation in the comments**. The audience here skews toward the patient end of [[AUDIENCE_PERSONA]] and tends to read past the first line.

That changes three things versus Instagram:

1. **We can say more.** Facebook tolerates and rewards longer, more narrative copy (§2). A post can carry the whole thought, not just the hook.
2. **We can link out.** A clickable link in the post body is allowed and useful here (§6) — Instagram can't do this; Facebook can.
3. **Hashtags barely matter.** Facebook is not a hashtag-discovery platform. The 8–12-tag Instagram norm is wrong here (§5).

What does **not** change: the [[SCROLL_TEST_PERSONA]] still has to want to stop. "Longer is allowed" is not "longer is the goal." Hold [[MISSION]] and the [[VOICE_DESCRIPTORS]] voice exactly as on every other channel. The job is to **land one idea well, in [[LOCALE]], for [[AUDIENCE_PAINS]]** — with room to breathe, not room to ramble.

## 2. Caption length

**Target: 100–250 words. Hard cap: 400 words.**

Facebook's longer canvas is a tool, not a license. A 120-word post that lands beats a 380-word post that wanders.

- **100–250 words** is the rhythm that reads as "a real person talking," with space for a short story, a hedged citation, and an invitation.
- **400 words** is the absolute ceiling for a single feed post. If a piece needs more, it is a **carousel, a Note, or a link to longform** — not one caption.
- Below ~80 words, ask whether this should just be an Instagram piece cross-posted; Facebook earns its place when the extra room is *used* for narrative, context, or a real link.

### Above the fold

Facebook truncates with a **"See more"** link after roughly the first **2–3 lines (~400–480 characters)** on most devices — **confirm the current truncation length at build time**, it drifts. Everything load-bearing — the hook and the reason to keep reading — must sit **before** "See more." If the reframe lives below the fold, it is buried.

### How to measure

Whitespace-split words. Hyphenated compounds count as one. Terms in any of [[LANGUAGES]] count as one word each. The link URL and any link-preview card text do **not** count toward the word total. When in doubt, paste into a word counter — don't eyeball.

## 3. Job count — pick 3 to 4, never all six

Every caption is built from some subset of these jobs. Facebook's longer canvas means it can carry **one more job than Instagram** — but not all of them.

1. **Hook** — the reframe, above the "See more" fold.
2. **Story / context** — the short narrative or specific moment the extra length is *for*. This is Facebook's signature job; it rarely fits on Instagram.
3. **Quote / source citation** — a founder, lineage, or canonical-source line, *with its source*, per [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]] and [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]].
4. **Evidence citation** — a hedged, named external reference, drawn only from [[SOURCE_ALLOWLIST]] (never [[SOURCE_DENYLIST]]) and limited to [[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]].
5. **Practical instruction** — *do this today*; one small, concrete action.
6. **CTA (optionally a link)** — an invitation tied to an offering from [[OFFERINGS]] or to [[CONTACT_WHATSAPP]] / [[CONTACT_INSTAGRAM]], in the [[CTA_STYLE]] register; may carry a clickable link (§6).

**Pick 3–4. Never all six.**

Doing everything at full length is the classic Facebook failure mode — the post becomes a newsletter nobody asked for. If you've written all six, the fix is not to trim each; it is to **drop two and, if the cut material is genuinely valuable, link to it** (§6).

Suggested pairings that work on Facebook:

- **Hook + story + soft CTA** (the highest performer for evergreen — Facebook's home turf).
- **Hook + story + link** (when the real depth lives on an owned page; tease here, deliver there).
- **Hook + evidence citation + practical instruction** (good for the credibility anchor when `[[RESEARCH_POST_MIN_PER_WEEK]]` > 0).
- **Hook + quote + CTA** (founder / canonical-source days; CTA soft, claims within [[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]]).

If a piece truly needs five or six jobs, **make it a carousel** (one job per card) or **split it** (post here, link out) — see §6.

## 4. Caption rhythm

- **First 2–3 lines must do the hook**, above the "See more" fold. If the reframe lives in paragraph two, you've buried it.
- **Short paragraphs, not a wall.** Facebook *allows* paragraphs (unlike the line-break-only Instagram beat), but keep them to 1–3 sentences with blank lines between. A single unbroken block is reject-on-sight even under the word cap.
- **No question stacking.** One opening question is fine; three in a row reads as anxious copywriting.
- **No "wait for it" / clickbait / engagement-bait** ("comment YES if…", "tag 3 friends", "share before it's gone"). Facebook actively penalizes engagement-bait, and it violates the [[VOICE_DESCRIPTORS]] voice. Keep to [[VOICE_DO]]; avoid [[VOICE_DONT]].
- **Reading level** stays at [[READING_LEVEL]].
- **Code-switching** across [[LANGUAGES]] is welcome where it sounds like [[LOCALE]]. Don't force it; don't avoid it.

### Reject on sight

- Hook hidden after the story, the quote, or the link.
- One unbroken wall of text, or essay paragraphs longer than ~3 sentences.
- Em-dash overload as a stylistic crutch (AI-slop tell — see `../brand_voice.md`, AI-slop section).
- Engagement-bait of any kind ("tag a friend," "comment below if," "share to win").
- Generic filler openers/closers ("In today's fast-paced world…", "Let's dive in…", "In conclusion…") and any phrasing in [[VOICE_DONT]] or [[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]].

## 5. Hashtag policy

**Facebook is not a hashtag platform. Use 0–3 hashtags, placed in the caption body (no first-comment requirement). Default to fewer; zero is a fully valid choice.**

This is the sharpest break from `./instagram.md`'s 8–12-tag, first-comment norm. Hashtags do little for reach on Facebook and a cloud of them reads as spam to this audience.

- **0–2 is the norm.** Use a tag only when it is a real, used community or campaign tag — e.g. one derived from [[BRAND_NAME]] / [[BRAND_SHORT_NAME]] or a live campaign for an [[OFFERING_NAME]] in [[OFFERINGS]].
- **3 is the hard ceiling.** More than three on Facebook is reject-on-sight.
- **No tier-stacking.** Do not port Instagram's broad/brand/local tier mix here.
- **No hashtag stuffing inside sentences** ("learn to #dothis today" — reject).
- **No empty trend-chasing tags** (generic motivation / viral / explore-page tags).
- **Founder / figure / lineage hashtags** must obey [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]] and [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]]: never an ambiguous standalone honorific that could be confused with another entity — qualify it so attribution is unmistakable.

> Note for the handoff bundle: the Publishing & Operations Agent still produces a tag set, but for Facebook it caps at 3 and places them in-body. The "first-comment hashtags" mechanic is an Instagram convention and does **not** apply here; on Facebook, the first comment is reserved for an optional **link** instead (§6).

## 6. Links — Facebook's distinctive capability

Unlike Instagram (links only in bio), **Facebook allows a clickable link directly in the post body.** Use it deliberately.

- **One link per post, maximum.** Two links splits attention and reads as a link dump.
- **Owned / allowlisted destinations only.** Link to a [[BRAND_NAME]] page, a calendar/resource, an [[OFFERING_NAME]] page, or a source on [[SOURCE_ALLOWLIST]]. **Never** link to a destination on [[SOURCE_DENYLIST]] or to any third-party page that implies an unapproved claim.
- **Two valid placements:**
  - **(a) Link-preview card in the body** — paste the URL, let Facebook render the preview card, then optionally delete the raw URL text so the card carries it cleanly. This is the default for a genuine "go read/sign-up here" post.
  - **(b) Link in the first comment** — keep the post itself image-led and drop the link as the first comment ("Details in the first comment 👇" — *if* that phrasing clears [[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]]). Use this when the image is the message and an in-body link would compete with it.
- **The link does not replace a CTA — it carries one.** The CTA text still obeys §8 ([[CTA_STYLE]], no urgency fabrication, claims within [[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]]).
- **A link does not license a longer post.** If you're linking *because* the depth lives elsewhere, the on-platform copy should get *shorter* (tease + link), not longer.
- **Truth still binds the preview.** The link-card title/description is brand-visible copy: it must honor [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]], must not touch [[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]], and must not reveal [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]]. If Facebook auto-pulls a misleading preview, fix it or use placement (b).

> Reach note: Facebook has historically dampened reach on posts with external links. Placement (b) is the mitigation when reach matters more than click-through. This is a tuning choice, not a rule — **confirm current link-reach behavior at build time** rather than trusting any fixed assumption.

## 7. Format: single image vs. carousel vs. link post vs. text

The 100–250 rule is for **single-image and text posts**. Other formats adjust it.

- **Single image** — 100–250 words, 3–4 jobs. Default for a self-contained idea.
- **Carousel / multi-photo** — caption 80–150 words (the cards do the heavy lifting). Card 1 = the hook image + one line; cards 2–N = one idea per card; last card = soft CTA. If 5–6 jobs need to land, this is the format. **Confirm the current Facebook multi-photo / carousel child max at build time.**
- **Link post** — caption 60–150 words (tease + context + CTA); the link card does the rest (§6). Shorter on purpose.
- **Text-only post** — allowed on Facebook (unlike Instagram), 100–250 words, for a pure story/announcement where no image earns its place. Use sparingly; a strong image almost always lifts a post.
- **Reel / video script** — out of launch for auto-publish; the studio **writes** Reel scripts (per PRD §9.1) but does not generate motion video. Manual handoff only.

> Channel availability: per PRD §16, **Instagram is the only auto-publish adapter at launch; Facebook is manual-handoff only** until a Facebook adapter is registered. These rules govern the drafted/queued artifact regardless of who presses publish. Aspect ratios are per-channel and Brand-Kit-resolved (PRD §11): a Facebook link card is typically **1.91:1**, while photo posts use **1:1 (1080×1080)** or **4:5 (1080×1350)**; base render ≥ **1080px** on the short edge. Confirm current Facebook image specs at build time.

## 8. Visual pairing

The **visual style guide** (`../visual_style_guide.md`) is canonical for all image decisions — load it alongside this guide for every Facebook piece. Below is the Facebook-specific summary; the full rule set lives there and in [[VISUAL_STRATEGY]] / [[VISUAL_REGISTER]] / [[VISUAL_VARIETY]].

- **Subject framing matches caption mood.** Pick the gesture/posture that embodies the post's idea; don't decorate a caption with generic, on-theme stock aesthetic.
- **[[LOCALE]] register.** Honor [[VISUAL_REGISTER]] and the [[LOCAL_DETAIL_BANK]] — the authentic, specific register reads better here than a generic one. Every image must differ from the previous 2–3 on ≥2 dimensions per [[VISUAL_VARIETY]] (e.g. gender / age / clothing / posture / setting / lighting mood).
- **Image generation is text-free.** Images are generated clean via [[IMAGE_PROVIDER]] — the Gemini-native default resolves through the stable token `gemini_image_pro` (the Gemini-3-era image+edit model, marketed as Nano Banana Pro; **confirm the marketing name and API model ID at build time** — do not trust the training cutoff), at quality tier [[IMAGE_QUALITY_TIER]]. A **deterministic Cloud Vision OCR text-free gate** runs on the raw pre-composite image — any baked glyphs → automatic fail → regenerate. We composite all brand typography ourselves ([[HEADLINE_FONT]] / [[LABEL_FONT]], palette [[PALETTE_HEX]], [[ACCENT_DARK_BG]] / [[ACCENT_LIGHT_BG]], with [[LOGO_ASSET]] / [[WORDMARK_TEXT]] as specified).
- **Anti-cues are required** in generation prompts — the explicit negatives from the visual style guide. **Rationed concept classes** are tracked: check the ledger before choosing a concept class so a rationed class isn't over-used across the feed (one feed, one ledger — Facebook and Instagram pieces share it per PRD §9.4).
- **Alt text is mandatory.** One sentence, descriptive, accessible — describe the actual scene (subject, dress, posture, light), not "[mood] vibes." Facebook supports custom alt text; always set it rather than relying on auto-generated alt.
- **No text-on-image clickbait.** All-caps "you won't believe…" style overlays → reject the whole piece.
- **Carousel card-1 overlays:** card 1 carries the hook image + a very short overlay (≤6 words) per the creative engine doc; the single-image overlay word-count rule does not govern carousel cards.

## 9. CTAs — what's allowed

CTAs on Facebook are gentle and **specific**, in the [[CTA_STYLE]] register — and may carry a link (§6).

- ✅ A small, concrete next action ("Try this for five minutes today.").
- ✅ A specific, real invitation tied to an [[OFFERING_NAME]] or a standing event in [[STANDING_WEEK]] at a named place in [[LOCALE]].
- ✅ A soft "Message us / [[CONTACT_WHATSAPP]] / [[CONTACT_INSTAGRAM]] if you'd like to learn more" for a relevant offering.
- ✅ A **clickable link** to an owned/allowlisted resource (in-body card or first comment, per §6).

- ❌ All-caps urgency ("MESSAGE US NOW!!!").
- ❌ Fear-of-missing-out / regret framing ("Don't miss out", "you'll regret it").
- ❌ Engagement-bait ("comment YES", "tag a friend", "share to win").
- ❌ Any urgency-fabricated or coercive CTA, and anything in [[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]]. The voice is invitational, not coercive (see `../brand_voice.md`).

One CTA per caption (it may include one link). Two CTAs reads desperate.

> Claims inside any CTA, link card, or body copy must stay within [[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]], never touch [[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]], honor [[COMPARATIVE_CLAIMS_ALLOWED]] and [[POLITICAL_CONTENT_ALLOWED]], and respect [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]] / [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]]. Quantitative claims follow the evidence rules in §3/§10.

## 10. Worked examples

Worked examples are **structural patterns**, not fixed copy — resolve them from [[SAMPLE_LINES_GOOD]] / [[SAMPLE_LINES_BAD]] and the active piece. The point is the *shape*, not the words.

### ✅ Passing pattern (≈180–220 words, 4 jobs: hook + story + practical instruction + soft CTA)

A passing single-image Facebook post:

- Opens with a 2–3 line **hook** that reframes a common assumption (above the "See more" fold).
- Tells one short, specific **story** — the narrative beat the extra Facebook length exists for — in 1–3-sentence paragraphs with blank lines between.
- Offers one **practical instruction** the reader can do today.
- Closes with one **soft, specific CTA** to a real [[OFFERING_NAME]] in [[LOCALE]] (optionally a single link per §6).
- Lands inside 100–250 words, at [[READING_LEVEL]], with 0–2 in-body hashtags (or none).

**Why it passes:** inside the word target; only 4 of the 6 jobs; hook above the fold; short-paragraph rhythm (not a wall); story is concrete and local; CTA specific and invitational; hashtag count Facebook-appropriate.

### ❌ Failing pattern (~520 words, all six jobs)

A failing post takes the same idea but piles on **all six jobs** — hook + story + quote + a second quote + evidence citation + practical instruction + CTA — run together as a wall of text, over the 400-word cap, with a fistful of Instagram-style hashtags pasted at the end.

**Why it fails:**

1. **Length.** Over the 400-word hard cap. Reject on length alone.
2. **Job overload.** Does everything at once — a newsletter, not a feed post. The fix is to *pick 3–4 and drop or link the rest* (§6), not to shorten each.
3. **Wall of text.** No paragraph breaks; nothing scannable above "See more."
4. **Wrong hashtag norm.** A 10-tag Instagram cloud on Facebook reads as spam (§5).
5. **Hook drowns.** The opening reframe may land, but the stacked citations bury it below the fold.

**Fix path.** Compress to hook + story + practical instruction + soft CTA (the passing pattern above). The extra quote and the evidence citation belong on a **carousel** (one per card), in a **linked longform** piece (§6), or on a different day — not in one Facebook caption. Cut the hashtags to 0–2.

## 11. Reviewer checklist (Creative Director use)

Run every Facebook submission through these in order. **First fail = reject; don't keep checking after that.** This sits inside the CD's **Gate 0 (Scroll Test) → Gate 1 (compliance)** pre-render review, with the **deterministic ledger-linter** (PRD §9.4) having already hard-blocked countable-rotation violations *before* this review, and the **CD post-render multimodal pass** checking the rendered artifact afterward. Safety fields ([[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]], [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]], [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]]) are **fail-closed**: empty or owner-unconfirmed in a relevant risk area → block and route to a human, never "nothing forbidden."

1. **Length.** 100–250 target, ≤400 hard cap. Carousel/link/text exceptions in §7.
2. **Above the fold.** Hook lands before "See more" (~first 2–3 lines / ~400–480 chars — confirm current truncation at build time).
3. **Jobs.** 3–4 of {hook, story, quote, evidence, instruction, CTA}. Six jobs = reject.
4. **Rhythm.** Short paragraphs (≤3 sentences), blank lines between; no wall of text; no question stacking; no AI-slop tells; reading level [[READING_LEVEL]].
5. **Voice & truth.** Forbidden phrasing ([[VOICE_DONT]] / [[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]])? Engagement-bait? Source paraphrased without attribution? Claim outside [[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]] or touching [[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]]? Quantitative claim not grounded per the deterministic claim-grounding rules (allowlist [[SOURCE_ALLOWLIST]], not [[SOURCE_DENYLIST]]; second source where [[REQUIRE_SECOND_SOURCE_FOR_QUANTITATIVE]]; reverify within [[CLAIM_REVERIFY_MONTHS]] months)? → see `../brand_voice.md`.
6. **Hashtags.** 0–3, in-body, Facebook-appropriate (not the Instagram tier-stack); founder/figure tags obey [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]].
7. **Link.** ≤1, to an owned/allowlisted destination (never [[SOURCE_DENYLIST]]); placement valid (in-body card or first comment, §6); link-card copy honors [[REQUIRED_FRAMING]] and reveals no [[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]].
8. **Visual pairing.** Subject + gesture + register match caption mood. Alt text present and concrete. Concept class named, differs from previous 2 posts per [[VISUAL_VARIETY]]; rationed-class ledger check done; text-free OCR gate passed.
9. **CTA.** ≤1, specific, invitational, in [[CTA_STYLE]]. No urgency fabrication, no engagement-bait.

Pass all nine → `approve`. Any fail → `revise` with the specific line note and the rule number. Round 3 → escalate the Managing Editor. **Compliant-but-dead is a reject.**
