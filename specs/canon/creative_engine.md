# The Creative Engine — [[BRAND_SHORT_NAME]]

> **Why this document exists.** Left ungoverned, an always-on feed collapses into a single caption skeleton (a stock reframe → an explanation → a CTA) and a single visual idea, and the *same* topic ships twice within a fortnight. Voice and channel-style guides keep a piece *compliant*; they do not keep the feed *varied*. This is the system that **generates variety** and refuses drab, samey, compliant-but-dead work.
>
> `brand_voice` and the per-channel style guides still govern. This document sits between them and the blank page — load all three before drafting. The **Content Ledger** (the `content_ledger` canon, in the Google Sheets/Drive system of record) is the memory that makes the rotation rules enforceable; the **deterministic ledger-linter** is what makes them non-negotiable.
>
> **Resolution.** Every `[[VARIABLE]]` resolves from the Brand Kit at prompt-assembly time. This generic doc never changes between brands — only its resolved values, the active hook/shape pack (keyed by `[[BRAND_TYPE]]`), and the attached Offering Briefs do. Illustrative examples below are **patterns**, not copy: regenerate the actual exemplars from `[[VOICE_DESCRIPTORS]]`, `[[VOICE_DO]]`, `[[SAMPLE_LINES_GOOD]]`, and `[[LOCAL_DETAIL_BANK]]`.

## 1. The one-idea rule

A caption carries **exactly one idea**. Not a topic — an *idea about* a topic.

- Topic: a broad subject area. (Too big. This produces drab summaries.)
- Idea: a single, specific claim or reframe about that topic that would make a stranger in `[[AUDIENCE_PERSONA]]` say "huh, I never thought of it that way."

If you can't state the idea in one sentence that earns that reaction, you don't have an idea yet. Go back to the angle lenses (§3), the Claim Bank / research bank, or the relevant Offering Brief (`[[OFFERINGS]]`) and find one.

Two ideas = a carousel or two posts. Never one caption.

## 2. Hook patterns — rotate, never repeat within 3 posts

The hook is the first 1–2 lines. It must survive the above-the-fold truncation (the channel's first-line cut). The ledger records which pattern each post used; **a pattern may not repeat within any 3 consecutive posts** across all agents (the feed is one feed — readers don't care which agent wrote it).

The **active pack is selected by `[[BRAND_TYPE]]`.** Ship both packs below; add more as real brands need them. Examples are structural templates — fill them from brand voice at resolve time.

### 2a. Editorial / Educational pack (default when `[[BRAND_TYPE]]` is education-led)

| # | Pattern | Structural template |
|---|---------|---------------------|
| H1 | **Myth-flip** | "You've been told to [common advice]. What if that's exactly the wrong instruction?" |
| H2 | **Research reveal** | "[N] studies were pooled on [topic]. The result wasn't a vibe — it was measurable." (VERIFIED Claim Bank entry only.) |
| H3 | **Specific moment** | A precise time + place + sensory beat drawn from `[[LOCAL_DETAIL_BANK]]`. |
| H4 | **Number contrast** | Two real numbers set against each other to reframe a default. |
| H5 | **Overheard line** | A quoted objection from `[[AUDIENCE_PERSONA]]`, then a one-line turn. |
| H6 | **Question that indicts the default** | A question that exposes a habit `[[AUDIENCE_PERSONA]]` never questions. |
| H7 | **Instruction-as-hook** | "Tonight, don't [the obvious thing]. Do this instead." |
| H8 | **Quote-reframe** | An authority/founder quote (cited) landed in `[[LOCALE]]` today. |
| H9 | **Season / local anchor** | A seasonal or place beat from `[[LOCAL_DETAIL_BANK]]` tied to the idea. |
| H10 | **Body-first** | A physical, do-it-now cue the reader can feel in their body. |
| H11 | **Contrast pair** | Two everyday facts juxtaposed to expose a gap. |
| H12 | **Honest curiosity gap** | A real, non-clickbait reason to keep reading, paid off in the body. |

### 2b. Product / Commerce pack (active when `[[BRAND_TYPE]]` is product/commerce-led)

| # | Pattern | Structural template |
|---|---------|---------------------|
| P1 | **Product-hero** | The product as the whole frame; one true benefit in words. |
| P2 | **UGC / social-proof** | A real customer's words/scene (vetted per research policy), framed honestly. |
| P3 | **Before → after** | The honest delta the product creates (no fabricated transformation). |
| P4 | **Founder story** | A specific, true origin beat tied to `[[MISSION]]`. |
| P5 | **Behind-the-scenes** | How it's actually made/done; one concrete detail. |
| P6 | **Launch / drop** | A new thing, stated plainly — truthful availability only. |
| P7 | **Tutorial** | One small, useful how-to the product enables. |
| P8 | **Testimonial** | A verified customer outcome with required hedges. |
| P9 | **Truthful-scarcity offer** | A genuine, dated offer — **never** fake urgency; honor `[[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]]` and `[[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]]`. |
| P10 | **Trend-jack** | A current moment, on-brand, only if it earns the idea (§1). |

Forbidden hook moves remain forbidden in both packs: clickbait, fear-bait, fake urgency, question-stacking, and anything in `[[VOICE_DONT]]` or `[[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]]`.

## 3. Angle lenses — how to find the idea

When a topic feels flat, pass it through these lenses until one produces an idea (§1):

- **The body's / product's side of the story.** What is physically or literally happening?
- **The objection.** What does the skeptic in `[[AUDIENCE_PERSONA]]` say? Answer *them* — turn a `[[AUDIENCE_PAINS]]` complaint into the hook.
- **The smallest possible version.** What's the 30-second version anyone could do in the middle of a `[[LOCALE]]` day?
- **The history.** What is the real origin / tradition / first principle here — and what's surprisingly practical about it?
- **The research.** What did a study actually measure? (Pull from the Claim Bank — VERIFIED entries only.)
- **The insider's-eye view.** What does someone who does this every day notice that an outsider misses?
- **The mis-translation.** What does everyone get wrong about this word or claim? (Confirm against the Claim Bank / canonical-facts; never bend a culturally or emotionally weighted term — see §5a.)
- **The local mirror.** Where does this topic already live in a `[[LOCALE]]` day? (Draw from `[[LOCAL_DETAIL_BANK]]`.)
- **The offering's promise.** What does `[[OFFERING_NAME]]` actually change for someone — stated accurately, never trivialized? (From the Offering Brief, `[[OFFERING_ID]]`.)

## 4. Caption shapes — no shape twice in a row

The recurring failure is one skeleton, every time. The ledger records the shape; **never the same shape twice in a row**, and the aphorism shape (S1, the easy default) is capped at **1 in any 5 posts**.

| # | Shape | Structure |
|---|-------|-----------|
| S1 | **Aphorism → practice** | Two-line reframe → 2–3 beats of meaning → one small action. (The easy default. Rationed.) |
| S2 | **Mini-story** | A person, a friction, a turn. 3 short paragraphs. Anonymous/composite, framed honestly. |
| S3 | **Research reveal** | Finding (hedged, cited) → what it means for an ordinary day → tiny action or soft CTA. |
| S4 | **Myth vs fact** | "Myth: …" / "What actually happens: …" — 2 beats each, no strawmen. |
| S5 | **List of three** | Three one-line beats on one idea. A rhythm, not a listicle. |
| S6 | **Dialogue** | Overheard objection → the honest answer. Conversational. |
| S7 | **Day anchor** | A specific time of day and what to do with it, anchored in `[[LOCAL_DETAIL_BANK]]`. |
| S8 | **Insider's note** | What a practitioner/maker notices, in first-person-plural institutional voice. |
| S9 | **Quote card caption** | ≤40 words. The image (typography) carries the line; the caption just lands it + CTA. |
| S10 | **Carousel narration** | 60–100 words; the slides do the work (see the channel style guide). |

> For a product/commerce brand, S2 (mini-story) carries founder/origin, S3 covers proof/results, S4 covers myth-busting, S8 covers behind-the-scenes, and S9/S10 carry product hero + tutorial. The rotation rules apply identically.

## 5. The specificity rule (anti-drab, non-negotiable)

Every caption must contain **at least one concrete, sensory, local detail** — a time, an object, a place, a number, a season. A category word is not a detail. A precise moment is. A vague "daily life" is not a detail; a specific scene from `[[LOCAL_DETAIL_BANK]]` is.

This is the single highest-leverage craft rule in this document. Generic copy is generic because nothing in it proves a human noticed something real. The Creative Director rejects any caption with zero specific details — **compliant-but-lifeless is a reject, not an approve.**

Draw concrete anchors from `[[LOCAL_DETAIL_BANK]]` (extend in agent memory; vary; don't exhaust the same few).

## 5a. The plain-speech guard

Specificity (§5) creates a new failure mode: stacking poetic fragments until a caption reads like a creative-writing exercise — "random jargon-gibberish, so hard to read." The specificity rule buys attention with REAL detail — it does not license literary performance. Hard rules:

1. **One detail that lands beats three stacked.** Pick the fragment that does the work; cut the rest. **Max ONE poetic fragment per caption.**
2. **Say it plainly to one real person.** Say every sentence as if to one real person in `[[AUDIENCE_PERSONA]]`, face to face. Any sentence you would never say out loud — cut or rewrite plainly. Honor `[[READING_LEVEL]]`.
3. **Close concrete.** The last line is the action or the image, never an abstraction dressed as profundity.
4. **Anchored references only.** Unintroduced placeholders ("this work", "the practice", "the courses") are banned. Name the offering — `[[OFFERING_NAME]]` — or drop the reference.
5. **Culturally weighted terms keep their true meaning.** Never bend a sacred, cultural, or emotionally loaded term into a loose metaphor. Honor `[[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]]` and the locale's conventions for `[[LOCALE]]`. Unsure? Check the canonical-facts / Claim Bank or ticket the Research & Verification Agent.

**Teaching pattern (generic):**
- Reject: a stack of sensitive-sounding fragments that no one would say aloud, ending on an abstraction.
- Pass: one concrete scene, plain sentences, closing on a single doable action.

## 6. Format rotation

Weekly mix across the whole feed (all agents combined; Publishing & Operations tracks). Honor `[[CHANNELS]]`, `[[POSTS_PER_WEEK_TARGET]]`, and `[[MAX_POSTS_PER_WEEK]]`:

- **Single image** — the workhorse, but **≤ ~50% of weekly posts.**
- **Carousel** — the natural home for research deep-dives, myth-vs-fact, how-tos, and "what is `[[OFFERING_NAME]]`" explainers. **Default to carousel for anything substantive.** Slide rules: slide 1 = hook image + a few words; one idea per slide; last slide = soft CTA.
- **Quote card** — typography-led (see the Visual Engine). **≤1 per week.**
- **Reel script** — write the script + shot list even though publishing is manual (static-assets-only studio). Hook in the first ~1.5 seconds. **≤1 per week** to start.

A recurring visual treatment (the same subject/treatment **label**) must not repeat back-to-back; see the rotation summary (§8).

## 7. Research-grounded minimum

Honors `[[RESEARCH_POST_MIN_PER_WEEK]]`, **including 0.**

- **When `[[RESEARCH_POST_MIN_PER_WEEK]]` > 0:** at least that many posts per week must be research-grounded (shape S3 or a research carousel), using only VERIFIED Claim Bank entries with their locked wording. This is the brand's credibility anchor. A week that misses the minimum is a miss, flagged in the weekly visibility digest.
- **When `[[RESEARCH_POST_MIN_PER_WEEK]]` = 0:** drop the standing research slot (remove any `research_grounded` flag), reallocate it, and **stop** ledger / Creative Director / digest enforcement of a research minimum. The Research & Verification Agent remains a constant role — it still vets any factual claim or testimonial that arises, per the research policy.

All factual claims must come from VERIFIED Claim Bank entries with locked wording regardless of the minimum; numeric fidelity is additionally enforced deterministically at the publish boundary (deterministic claim-grounding).

## 8. Rotation rules — summary (the deterministic ledger-linter enforces; CD verifies)

These **countable** rules are hard-blocked by the deterministic ledger-linter (which reads the Sheets ledger) **before** Creative Director review:

1. One idea per caption (§1).
2. Hook pattern: no repeat within 3 consecutive posts (§2).
3. Caption shape: no repeat back-to-back; S1 (aphorism) ≤ 1 per 5 posts (§4).
4. Specificity: ≥ 1 concrete local detail per caption (§5).
5. Idea: no re-run of the same core idea within 30 days (ledger check by topic → idea sentence).
6. Visual treatment **label**: no back-to-back repeat (§6). (Rendered-image "visibly different" judgment is **not** in the linter — it routes to the CD post-render multimodal pass. There is deliberately no rigid subject/age/clothing/posture/lighting hard-block; that would fight the variety principle.)
7. Research post: meets `[[RESEARCH_POST_MIN_PER_WEEK]]` (§7), when > 0.

## 9. The two tests before any submit

- **Say-it-aloud test:** read the caption aloud as if to one real person in `[[SCROLL_TEST_PERSONA]]`. Any line you would not say out loud — rewrite it.
- **Scroll test:** look at hook + image at thumbnail size for two seconds. Would `[[SCROLL_TEST_PERSONA]]` stop? If the honest answer is "maybe," the answer is no.

## 10. Draft document discipline

Do not bury the caption under self-congratulating checklist tables. A draft document contains, in order:

1. Idea sentence (one line) + ledger fields (topic / hook pattern / shape / visual treatment label / format / **language**).
2. The caption.
3. First-comment hashtags.
4. Visual brief (MESSAGE → FEELING → TREATMENT [+ image, words, light-mood], per the Visual Engine) + alt text owner note.
5. **One** compact compliance block — max 8 lines, only the items that required judgment. No ✓-theater tables.

Draft review runs through a **child review task** assigned to the Creative Director (not an inline confirmation prompt). The status flip on the child task is the verdict; a **blocked-by** link on the draft is the waiting path (it auto-wakes when the review clears).

## 11. Captions — image-first

On a visual feed almost no one reads the caption — the **image / carousel is the message.** The caption is a *secondary* layer; the content does NOT live here.

- **The image already said it — don't repeat it.** The caption adds ONE thing the picture couldn't: a deeper line, a reframe, a question, a piece of context. If it just narrates the slides, cut it.
- **The first line IS the caption for most people.** It shows above the fold and must stand alone — a hook or a complete thought. Front-load it; never bury it behind a throat-clear.
- **Short — a hard cap.** First line (the hook) + AT MOST 2–3 short body lines + the CTA + hashtags. Past ~3 short body lines you are explaining — cut it. A six-paragraph caption is an automatic revise. For a **carousel, shorter still** — the slides teach; the caption is a one-line frame + the CTA.
- **One idea, plain speech** (§1, §5a still bind).
- **Close with the soft CTA from `brand_assets`.** Use `[[CTA_STYLE]]`: name `[[OFFERING_NAME]]`, invite a DM / `[[CONTACT_WHATSAPP]]` / `[[CONTACT_INSTAGRAM]]`. NEVER use any phrase in `[[CTA_FORBIDDEN_PHRASES]]`.
- **Hashtags: a tight set (~6–10)** — brand + topic + local (drawn from `[[BRAND_NAME]]`, the idea's topic, and `[[LOCALE]]`). Never a 30-tag wall. End of caption or first comment. The channel style guide governs exact count.
- **Alt text on every image** (accessibility + reach) — authored in the pipeline.
- **Non-disclosure + research bind captions too:** honor `[[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]]`; factual claims only from VERIFIED Claim Bank entries with locked wording, within `[[CLAIMS_ALLOWED]]` and never in `[[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]]`; apply `[[REQUIRED_FRAMING]]`.

The Creative Director revises on any caption that re-narrates the image, runs long, buries the first line, or uses a forbidden CTA phrase.

## 12. Format decision — carousel or single image (decide consciously, EVERY time)

A deliberate choice the writer makes per idea — never a default, never an afterthought. State which and why in the draft.

- **Carousel** when the post TEACHES or tells a story across beats: steps, a why → how, myth → fact, "3 signs…", a small lesson. Captions go unread, so the teaching has to live ACROSS the slides — this is the format that delivers real value and builds genuine belief in `[[OFFERINGS]]`. **Default to carousel for anything substantive.**
- **Single image** when one frame is the whole punch: a pure hook, a relatable feeling, a cited quote, a single research stat, an announcement, or a `product_led` hero shot.
- **Rule of thumb:** if making the point land would need more than one line of text on the image, it's a carousel.

If the engine produces single images for everything, that is a bug, not a style.

## 13. Pipeline & rendering notes (for the writer)

- The chosen **language** is set at IDEATE+DRAFT (default = primary = first entry of `[[LANGUAGES]]`), recorded on the Draft and the ledger row. For multi-language brands without a per-slot pin, the Managing Editor treats language as an additional **variety axis** across the week.
- Images are generated **text-free** (clean space reserved in the lower band) via `[[IMAGE_PROVIDER]]` (default: the Gemini-native image model — confirm the current marketing name and API ID at build time), at the `[[IMAGE_QUALITY_TIER]]` floor; brand typography is composited **after** generation, never model-baked. A **Cloud Vision OCR text-free gate** verifies the generated photo carries no baked-in text before compositing.
- The Creative Director runs a **pre-render** pass (Gate 0 Scroll Test + Gate 1 Compliance, including the fail-closed safety fields) on the draft, and a **post-render multimodal** pass on the finished artifact (quality bar + non-disclosure-in-image + "visibly different").
- **Safety is fail-closed:** if a relevant safety field (`[[CLAIMS_FORBIDDEN]]`, `[[NON_DISCLOSURE_RULES]]`, `[[REQUIRED_FRAMING]]`) is empty or owner-unconfirmed, a piece in that risk area is **blocked and routed to a human** — never treated as "nothing forbidden." Honor `[[COMPARATIVE_CLAIMS_ALLOWED]]` and `[[POLITICAL_CONTENT_ALLOWED]]`. Research sources obey `[[SOURCE_ALLOWLIST]]` / `[[SOURCE_DENYLIST]]`, `[[CLAIM_REVERIFY_MONTHS]]`, and `[[REQUIRE_SECOND_SOURCE_FOR_QUANTITATIVE]]`.
